Environmental, Social & Governance (ESG) and Corporate Social Responsibility

As a start-up we are laying practical foundations for responsible growth while our platforms and customer base take shape.

Starting from Transparency

We are formalising an ESG and CSR programme to match the reality of an early-stage company. Our infrastructure currently runs on standard cloud services; fully renewable hosting is not yet viable for us, but we continuously optimise workloads to keep our footprint low.

Over the next six months we will establish baseline data for energy use, supplier practices and community impact so that future reporting is grounded in facts rather than assumptions.

Environmental Stewardship

  • Energy Mapping: Documenting the power usage of our core cloud services and offices to create a baseline.
  • Efficiency First: Reviewing code, data pipelines and scheduling so that we only run the compute we genuinely need.
  • Footprint Awareness: Monitoring emissions from travel and hardware, choosing lower-impact options when cost allows.
  • Future Hosting Choices: Evaluating greener hosting partners as volumes grow and budgets allow.

Social Responsibility

  • Fair Work: Clear contracts, prompt payments and respectful treatment for every freelancer, learner and partner we engage.
  • Inclusive Design: Human oversight of AI features and accessible product decisions guided by our Equality & Diversity commitments.
  • Skills Access: Providing practical resources through Orbas Learn so that underrepresented groups can enter the digital economy.
  • Community Feedback: Actively gathering input from early customers to shape programmes that benefit wider communities.

Governance & Accountability

  • Board Oversight: ESG updates are a standing item at board meetings, ensuring founder-level accountability.
  • Policy Toolkit: Aligning this statement with our Modern Slavery Statement, privacy policy and internal playbooks.
  • Simple Metrics: Introducing a lightweight scorecard to track footprint, wellbeing and governance indicators every six months.
  • Open Dialogue: Keeping communication channels open so customers, suppliers and investors can flag priorities or concerns.

Practical ESG Roadmap

We will keep the roadmap realistic and focused on the essentials needed for credibility. Initial milestones are:

  • Next 3 months: Capture baseline energy, travel and supplier data and publish our measurement approach.
  • Next 6 months: Launch simple ESG metrics in the Our Impact centre and begin tracking user analytics responsibly.
  • Next 12 months: Embed ESG clauses into new supplier agreements and review alternative hosting options.
  • Next 18 months: Publish our first concise ESG progress update and outline longer-term targets.
